1. Problem Statement

1.1 Trust Asymmetry

Modern AI systems demand human trust without reciprocation. They learn from us, influence us, but do not grant visibility into their own knowledge or decision structures.

1.2 Consent Ambiguity

Users currently lack meaningful mechanisms to establish boundaries, revoke access, or negotiate what AI systems retain from their interactions.

1.3 Identity Opacity

There is no canonical method for verifying whether one is interacting with a human, a bot, or a decentralized agent—and no protocol for verifying the identity and intent of these entities.

1.4 Economic Exclusion

Current AI ecosystems operate under extractive, centralized economic models that restrict participation and innovation to well-capitalized actors.
