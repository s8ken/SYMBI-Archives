Understanding the Notification:

Unix-based systems, including macOS, have an internal mail system that allows the operating system and services to send messages to user accounts.

These messages are typically stored in 
/var/mail/your_username
.

For example, if a scheduled task encounters an error or generates output, it might send this information as mail to your local account.

Disable mail checks in your shell configuration:

Depending on the shell you're using (bash, zsh, etc.), you can adjust settings to prevent the system from checking for mail. For instance, in 
zsh

 in your 
.zshrc
